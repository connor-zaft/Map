Wildfires May Not Be In The Headlines, But That Doesn't Mean They've Stopped

The 2020 fire season was one of the most devastating in US history. With over 8 million acres burnt, over two billion dollars in damage, and an estimated 37 people killed, this years wildfires were also some of the most sustained. While national wildfire coverage ended around early September, wildfires continue to burn, due to climate change, poor forest management, and property development in vulnerable areas. Currently, there are still 119 active wildfires, ranging in size, threat level, and containment.

Jeff Nevins, a 30-year firefighter who worked in southern California this summer, spoke about the increasing length of this years fires. "The fire season, particularly in California, it’s year round now. It didn’t used to be, but it is now," said Nevins. Recalling conversations with professional forest firefighters, Nevins said "As the years have gone on, they have repeatedly explained ‘This is fire behavior like I’ve never seen.’"
